Daily Mindfulness: 3 minutes of mindful listening

I persuaded my partner to take part in the 3 minute mindful listening exercise at the end of an evening. David was very happy to spend his three monologue in silence. For me those were three long, frustrating minutes. I found the exercise very difficult because I tend to fill every waking moment -- and some of my sleeping moments -- with information. Silencing my inner chatter and just being still is something I struggle with. The exercise led to a good conversation about listening and attentiveness in different contexts, and what it means to give someone else space to talk... or not talk!

I plan tp practice listening, dipping and looping next time I'm in a meeting to see if I can remain fully engaged and give my full attention for a sustained period of time through consciously applying these techniques.
